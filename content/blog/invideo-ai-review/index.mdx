## What InVideo AI is (in one sentence)

InVideo is a template-driven editor with AI-assisted workflows designed for **ads, social videos and recurring content**—where speed and consistency matter more than film-grade editing.

## Who InVideo is best for

- creators producing multiple videos per week (Reels/Shorts, promos, list videos)
- ecommerce teams who need **many variations** (hooks, CTAs, angles)
- marketers who want a repeatable template system rather than a complex timeline editor

## The #1 mistake: letting templates stay “template-y”

Templates are a starting point. If you keep stock clips and generic typography, your output will look like everyone else’s.

To fix that, treat InVideo like a *draft engine*:

1. Replace stock media with your own footage whenever possible.
2. Apply a brand kit (fonts, colors, logo, lower thirds).
3. Write hooks that sound like a human (not “marketing copy”).
4. Keep one idea per video (one offer, one CTA).

## A practical workflow for performance marketing

Use this system to A/B test without burning hours:

1. **Angle**: pick one promise (save time, save money, look better, reduce risk).
2. **Hooks**: write 3 hooks (curiosity, pain, result).
3. **Body**: 3 beats max (problem → proof → solution).
4. **CTA**: 2 CTAs (soft “learn more” + direct “try now”).
5. Export 6–10 variants, test, then iterate only on winners.

## Plan/limits: what to verify (April 2026)

InVideo’s plan names and quotas can change, but the Help Center explains the **credit system** clearly (updated March/April 2026).

### Individual plans (credits + quotas)

As of April 2026, invideo’s Help Center describes individual tiers like **Plus**, **Max**, and **Generative**:

- **Plus**: 100 credits/mo, 100 iStock, 20 GB storage, 4 AI avatars & voice clones
- **Max**: 400 credits/mo, 200 iStock, 100 GB storage, 16 AI avatars & voice clones
- **Generative**: 1,000 credits/mo, 1,000 iStock, 2 TB storage, 40 AI avatars & voice clones

It also states credits reset monthly and unused credits don’t roll over—plan your month to avoid waste.

### How credits translate to output

The same Help Center article gives an example for a **1‑minute video**:

- **Basic**: 2 credits/minute (good for stock-based drafts)
- **Pro**: 80 credits/minute
- **Ultra**: 160 credits/minute

That’s why the plan choice depends on your output quality:

- If you mostly ship **Basic** drafts, credits stretch far.
- If you need **Pro/Ultra** often, the math quickly pushes you toward higher tiers.

Before subscribing, also check:

- watermark rules on the entry tier
- export/resolution limits (HD/4K where applicable)
- included media libraries (if you rely on stock)
- voice/subtitle quotas if you use them heavily
