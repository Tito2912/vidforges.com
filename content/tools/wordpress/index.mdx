## What WordPress is

WordPress is a content management system (CMS) used for blogs, media sites and business websites. For an affiliate SEO cluster, WordPress is often chosen because it supports:

- a strong editorial workflow (drafts, updates, categories)
- scalable internal linking and content hubs
- flexible templates and plugins (when used carefully)

## WordPress.com vs self-hosted (how to choose)

- **Hosted (WordPress.com)**: faster setup, fewer technical tasks, fewer moving parts.
- **Self-hosted (WordPress.org)**: maximum flexibility and control, but you manage hosting, updates and security.

If your priority is speed and simplicity, hosted is usually the safest starting point. If you need deep customization, self-hosting can make sense.

## Pricing snapshot (checked April 2026)

WordPress.org (self-hosted) is free software, but you pay for **hosting**, **domain**, and sometimes premium themes/plugins.

WordPress.com (hosted) bundles hosting + features. As of April 2026, the official pricing page (USD view) shows paid plans like:

- **Personal**: $9/mo on monthly billing (lower with longer commitments shown on the pricing page)
- **Premium**: $18/mo on monthly billing
- **Business**: $40/mo on monthly billing
- **Commerce**: $70/mo on monthly billing

Exact pricing varies by currency, billing term, and promotions—verify your current checkout before subscribing.

## SEO cluster structure (practical)

For long-term SEO, build a simple architecture:

- 1–3 **hub pages** (the main topic/category)
- 5–20 **supporting articles** per hub (how-to, comparisons, FAQs)
- consistent internal links: support → hub and hub → best pages

## Performance & Core Web Vitals basics

Keep your site fast by default:

- choose a lightweight theme
- avoid plugin overload
- compress images and keep hero sections simple
- use caching/CDN features from your host (when available)
