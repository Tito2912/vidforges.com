  <h2>How to choose a tool (SEO-friendly checklist)</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Output first:</strong> ads, tutorials, UGC-style creatives, or localization (each needs a different stack).</li>
    <li><strong>Control vs speed:</strong> shot-level control (generators) vs template speed (template editors).</li>
    <li><strong>Brand consistency:</strong> can you lock typography, colors, and pacing across variants?</li>
    <li><strong>Compliance & licensing:</strong> avoid sensitive claims and keep references/rights clean.</li>
    <li><strong>Workflow fit:</strong> versioning, approvals, exports, and how you’ll ship weekly.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>FAQ</h2>
  <div class="faq-grid">
    <details class="faq-item">
      <summary>
        <h3>What’s the minimum stack to publish consistently?</h3>
        <span class="icon">+</span>
      </summary>
      <div class="faq-content">
        <p>A generator for shots, an editor for captions/pacing/sound, and a simple checklist. Most “performance” comes from the edit and iteration loop.</p>
      </div>
    </details>
    <details class="faq-item">
      <summary>
        <h3>How do I avoid generic AI-looking videos?</h3>
        <span class="icon">+</span>
      </summary>
      <div class="faq-content">
        <p>Use references (product shots, brand assets), keep the first 3 seconds sharp, and standardize captions, pacing, and music. Iterate one change at a time.</p>
      </div>
    </details>
    <details class="faq-item">
      <summary>
        <h3>Should I pick one tool and go “all in”?</h3>
        <span class="icon">+</span>
      </summary>
      <div class="faq-content">
        <p>Start with one workflow and one main tool, but keep an editor as the stable layer. Mixing tools is normal when you need speed (templates) and control (shot generation).</p>
      </div>
    </details>
