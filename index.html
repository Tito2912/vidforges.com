Kling comes from Kuaishou’s video research team—the same group powering Kwai and KMovie. The infrastructure is built for daily short-form production, so renders stay stable under load.

Physics-aware motion

The model tracks camera trajectories and body mechanics, letting you script pushes, pans, or dolly shots without rubbery motion. Prompt weight sliders keep subjects anchored instead of drifting.

Official releases advertise up to 2 minute, 1080p 30 fps clips. Pair that with Kling’s queue view to batch variants and pick the cleanest take for delivery.

Under the hood

How Kling AI works

Recent Kling 2.x releases combine a diffusion transformer (DiT) with an in-house 3D VAE for spatiotemporal compression, keeping detail on fast motion while staying efficient enough for the public queue.

  • Diffusion Transformer core paired with a 3D VAE so camera moves, reflections, and cloth motion stay coherent.
  • Standard, Pro, and Master quality modes in Kling 2.x with 720p or 1080p outputs at 30 fps for clips up to ~2 minutes.
  • Engine built by Kuaishou’s video lab (Kwai/KMovie), so the queue and scaling mirror daily short-form production needs.

Why Kling AI

  • Cinematic prompts out of the box: spell out camera moves, lens, and pacing inside the prompt and Kling preserves depth plus lighting for a believable shot.
  • Hybrid inputs that stick: mix text, reference stills, or base footage to keep brand styling and character continuity across variants.
  • Queue + versioning built in: monitor job progress, duplicate a render with one tweak, and compare results without leaving the dashboard.

FAQ

What AI model powers Kling?
Kling 2.x runs a diffusion transformer (DiT) paired with a custom 3D VAE for spatiotemporal compression. It offers Standard, Pro, and Master modes with 720p/1080p outputs at 30 fps, and official releases have showcased clips up to roughly two minutes.
What resolutions are supported?
Options evolve. Common HD and vertical outputs are typically available; confirm in the live interface.
